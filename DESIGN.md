# The Talon's Shadow — Design North Star

*Written day 5 of the jam. The jam ships the PULSE plus one taste of one tree.
This doc is where the full game starts from — not from memory.*

## The soul (locked)

A grounded third-person rogue whose **attention has a body** — the crow. You
extend your awareness out through it, read the world, and return to commit.
The pulse of out-and-in is the game. Hard constraint: the crow must never
feel like a mode or a menu. The instant it does, the flow is dead.

## The base pulse (every player has this)

- **Send** the crow to a perch — your attention placed in the world
- **Peek** through its eyes — the world runs slower than thought (time dilation)
- **Perch-hop** — send again *while peeking* and the crow re-routes from its
  own vantage. Deep scouting with a rising cost: every hop leaves your rooted
  body in the open a beat longer. Canon since the jam (discovered in play,
  day 5): base pulse, not tree content — it's the connective tissue all three
  trees hop along (hop→Step = traveled teleports, hop→distract→hop = herding,
  hop→look = chained vantage)
- **Return** — release always snaps home, sharper than it left
- Slow-mo and the rooted/vulnerable body are not abilities — they're the
  cost-and-texture of the pulse itself, tuned, never unlocked

## Three trees — three uses of attention

How you use the crow is who you are. Each tree deepens ONE facet of the same
pulse; none adds a new resource or a new mode.

### SIGHT — what the crow sees
The scout's tree. Peek stops being a periscope and becomes revelation.
- Marked enemies visible through walls while extended
- **Weakness sight**: armor seams, opening tells, crit windows revealed from
  crow height only
- Secret passages / hidden paths shimmer from the vantage
- Capstone — **True Nature**: the crow sees what things really are: the elite
  disguised as fodder, the false wall, the NPC's actual allegiance

### STEP — where the crow lets you be
The assassin's tree. Attention becomes presence.
- **Shadow Step** (the jam ships this): dash while extended → become where
  your attention is; the perch is CONSUMED — you can't keep watching from the
  place you now stand
- Step upgrades: strike-from-arrival (teleport-attack), longer range, a
  second charge
- Tandem combos: dash-to-crow chains, crow repositions mid-step
- Capstone — **Murmuration**: brief moment where body and crow swap freely

### VOICE — what the crow says
The conductor's tree. The crow speaks; the world reacts.
- **Distraction call** (jam ships the seed: enemies stalk a fresh perch)
- Directed lures: send enemies TO a spot, not just away from you
- Combat calls: interrupt a windup, break a guard, panic a pack
- Capstone — **The Parliament**: call a flock; the sky answers

## Design rules for all trees

1. Everything routes through send/peek/return — no ability bypasses the pulse
2. Costs are situational (perch consumed, position revealed, cooldown), never
   a mana bar — meters ration; rhythm flows
3. Each tree must change how a player approaches the SAME encounter — the
   test of build identity is the same room played three ways

## Jam→full-game bridge

| In the jam | Full-game seed |
|---|---|
| Peek + slow-mo | base pulse |
| Perch-hopping while extended | base pulse — the trees' connective tissue |
| Perch distraction (edge-trigger) | VOICE tree root |
| Shadow Step (perch consumed) | STEP tree root |
| Reticle gold-on-perch | SIGHT tree root (the eye that knows where it can land) |

## Open questions (carry into full-game pre-production)

- What does the player SPEND to grow a tree? (kills? secrets found? feathers?)
- Rooted-while-peeking: current answer is "rooted but you can Step out" —
  revisit whether drift-while-peeking serves flow better once combat is denser
- Does the crow have its own health/risk, or is it untouchable attention?
- Stamina/cost question from day 4: resolved as "no meter — situational costs
  only" (rule 2). Revisit only if playtests show peek-spam trivializing fights.
